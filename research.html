BNIRA Lab

Research

Our research bridges neural engineering, signal processing, and artificial intelligence to address fundamental and translational challenges in biomedical science.

Research Philosophy

At BNIRA Lab, we believe that understanding the brain and nervous system through quantitative methods can yield transformative solutions for medicine. Our approach is inherently interdisciplinary, drawing from electrical engineering, computer science, physiology, and clinical medicine.

We pursue both fundamental research (how does the brain encode information?) and applied research (how can we use that knowledge to build better assistive devices and diagnostics?). Student-led projects form the core of our lab's output.

Research Areas

🧠

Brain–Computer Interfaces (BCI)

Neural Decoding · Assistive Technology · Rehabilitation

We develop non-invasive BCI systems based on EEG that allow users to control external devices through thought alone. Current projects focus on motor imagery classification, P300-based spellers, and SSVEP paradigms. We design end-to-end pipelines from signal acquisition through real-time classification.

Motor Imagery P300 SSVEP Transformer Real-Time Classification
📈

Neural Signal Processing

EEG · EMG · ECG · Feature Extraction

High-quality signal processing is the foundation of all our downstream tasks. We investigate artifact removal, advanced filtering, time-frequency representations (STFT, wavelet, Hilbert-Huang), and feature extraction strategies for EEG, EMG, and ECG signals.

Wavelet Transform ICA CSP Artifact Rejection
🤖

Biomedical AI & Machine Learning

Deep Learning · Clinical AI · Diagnostic Systems

We apply and advance deep learning methods — CNNs, LSTMs, Transformers, and graph neural networks — for biomedical classification, anomaly detection, and prognostics. Special emphasis is placed on interpretability and robustness for clinical deployment.

CNN LSTM Explainable AI Transfer Learning
💊

Neurological Disorder Diagnosis

Epilepsy · Parkinson's · Mental Health · Biomarkers

We develop computational biomarkers and automated diagnostic tools for neurological and psychiatric conditions. Current focus areas include seizure detection from EEG, tremor quantification for Parkinson's disease, and EEG-based mental workload and stress assessment.

Seizure Detection Parkinson's Mental Workload
⌚

Wearable & Embedded Biosensing

IoT · Edge AI · Low-Power Design

Translating lab-based algorithms to real-world hardware is a key priority. We design low-power wearable systems using microcontrollers and SoCs, implementing compressed AI models for on-device biosignal inference — enabling continuous, ambulatory health monitoring.

Arduino / STM32 TensorFlow Lite BLE Model Quantization
